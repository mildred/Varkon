Surfaces

A surface has two parametric directions, U and V and consists of one or more patches in each direction. A surface with 2 patches in the U direction and 3 patches in the V direction thus has a total of 6 patches. Similar to curves, the number of patches in a surface depends on what type of surface it is and how you create it.

A surface has two sides, positive and negative. When displayed with color in a OpenGL window the positive side of a surface will become bright and shiny while the negative side will become dark and dull. Which is which depends on how you create the surface.

Methods

  • Bounded plane
  • Ruled
  • Cylinder
  • Rotational
  • Sweep
  • Offset
  • Approx

MBS includes many more surface methods. See the MBS entity creation index for more info.
